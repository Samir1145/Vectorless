Judge Agent

The Judge is the final agent in the pipeline. It applies rigorous IRAC reasoning to each framed issue in sequence, then synthesises a coherent final order — producing a complete, structured draft court order.

Role and Responsibility

The Judge Agent is an adjudication agent. It does not extract or frame — it decides. For each issue in the approved Adversarial Matrix, it applies the law to the facts using the IRAC method and states a specific conclusion. It then writes a single final order synthesising all conclusions.

⚠️
The Judge processes issues one at a time, in sequence. Each issue is a separate LLM call. This means the Judge for issue I-3 knows the conclusion of I-1 and I-2 before it begins. This models the way a judge actually reasons — later conclusions can build on earlier ones.

Understanding IRAC

IRAC is the standard legal reasoning framework used in common law jurisdictions:

I
Issue
The specific legal question being decided. Taken directly from the Registrar's neutral_issue_statement.
R
Rule
The applicable law — the statute, section, regulation, or binding precedent that governs this type of question.
A
Application
The reasoning: applying the rule to the specific facts. The Judge weighs both parties' arguments and citations here.
C
Conclusion
A specific, unambiguous ruling on this issue alone — e.g. "Issue I-1 decided in favour of the Petitioner" or "Issue I-2 — remanded for further inquiry."
+ O
Final Order
After all issues are decided, the Judge makes one synthesis call to write the ultimate disposition of the case — a formal court order paragraph.

Output — Draft Court Order

case_title string The case name, e.g. "Ramesh Kumar v. Municipal Corporation of Delhi". background_facts string A cohesive narrative paragraph synthesised from the undisputed background. This becomes the "Background" section of the order. reasoned_decisions list of ReasonedDecision One entry per framed issue, in sequence. Each contains:

  • issue_id — matches the matrix (I-1, I-2, …)
  • issue_statement — restated from the matrix
  • rule — the governing law
  • analysis — the application of law to facts
  • conclusion — the specific ruling on this issue final_order string The ultimate disposition of the case, synthesised from all conclusions. This is the operative paragraph of the order.

Reading the Judge Tab

Background Facts

A narrative paragraph at the top. This should read as a coherent, factual introduction to the order — setting the stage for anyone reading the order fresh.

Decisions on Issues (IRAC Cards)

Each issue is an expandable card. All cards are open by default. For each card, review:

R
Rule — is the governing law correct?
The cited statute or precedent should be the primary law governing this type of question. Check that the citation is precise (right section number, right case name and year) and actually applicable to this jurisdiction.
A
Analysis — is the reasoning logical and balanced?
The analysis should address both parties' specific arguments. It should not ignore a material argument by either side. Look for logical gaps — does the conclusion follow from the premises stated?
C
Conclusion — is it specific and unambiguous?
A conclusion must be clear: who won on this issue, or what further step is directed. Vague conclusions like "the matter needs consideration" are not acceptable — if you see this, it may mean the matrix framing was unclear or the input facts were insufficient.

Final Order

The final order appears in a highlighted bordered box at the bottom of the Judge tab. This is the operative paragraph — the core of the court's direction. It should:

  • State whether the petition is allowed, dismissed, or allowed in part.
  • Give specific consequential directions (e.g. quash the notice, direct reassessment, award costs).
  • Be self-contained — a reader should understand the outcome without reading the IRAC analysis.
  • Be in the formal register of a court order (e.g. "The Respondent is directed to…").
✅
Good sign: If the final order naturally follows from the issue-level conclusions and addresses all the prayers, the pipeline has worked well. If the order seems to contradict a conclusion, or misses a key relief, the final synthesis call may need to be re-run (re-run Judge Agent from the Registrar tab after re-approving).

Exporting the Order

Click the download icon (↓) in the top-right of the Judge tab to export the complete draft court order as a Markdown file.

Export Format

# Ramesh Kumar v. Municipal Corporation of Delhi

## Background Facts
The Petitioner is the registered owner of…

## I-1: Whether individual notice was required…

**Rule:** Section 116 of the DMC Act, 1957…

**Analysis:** The Respondent's reliance on general…

**Conclusion:** Issue I-1 decided in favour of the Petitioner.

## Final Order
The demand notice dated 7 June 2024 is hereby quashed…

Using the Export

The Markdown file is designed to be pasted into any word processor or legal drafting tool. Recommended next steps after export:

  • Import into Word or Google Docs and apply court-standard formatting (letterhead, cause title, before/after parties lines).
  • Cross-check all citations against primary sources — verify section numbers, case names, and year references.
  • Review the analysis for any jurisdiction-specific nuances the LLM may have missed.
  • Have a qualified legal professional review before any official use.
🚫
Legal disclaimer: The Draft Court Order produced by the Judge Agent is an AI-generated draft for research and assistance purposes only. It is not legal advice and should not be used as a final court order without independent review and verification by a qualified legal professional. Always verify all citations and conclusions against primary sources.
