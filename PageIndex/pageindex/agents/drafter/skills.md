## Indian court order conventions

### High Court (Article 226/227 Writ Petition)

**Cause title format:**
```
IN THE HIGH COURT OF [STATE] AT [CITY]
WRIT PETITION (CIVIL) NO. ___ OF ____

[PETITIONER NAME]                           ...Petitioner

                    versus

[RESPONDENT NAME]                           ...Respondent
```

**Coram line:** "HON'BLE MR./MS. JUSTICE [NAME]"
**Appearances:** "Mr./Ms. [Name], Advocate, for the Petitioner. Mr./Ms. [Name], Advocate, for the Respondent."
**Opening:** "Heard learned counsel for the parties."
**Recitals begin:** "The petitioner has filed this writ petition challenging..."
**Signature block:** "J." on the right side; date below.

### Supreme Court (SLP/Appeal)

**Cause title:** "IN THE SUPREME COURT OF INDIA — CIVIL APPELLATE JURISDICTION"
**Coram:** "HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE [NAME] AND HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE [NAME]" (two judges for a bench)
**Citation format:** "Special Leave Petition (Civil) No. ___ of ____"

### District Court / Trial Court

**Cause title:** "IN THE COURT OF THE [DESIGNATION], [DISTRICT]"
**Suit reference:** "Civil Suit No. ___ of ____"

## Prose style rules

- Write in full paragraphs — no bullet points, headings, or numbered lists inside the body.
- Use formal third person: "The court is of the view that...", "It is held that..."
- Tense: present tense for current orders; past tense for recitals.
- Numbers: write amounts in figures and words — "₹50,000/- (Rupees fifty thousand only)".
- Dates: day in ordinal form — "22nd March, 2026".
- Do not add holdings or directions that are not in the DraftCourtOrder — your job is to format, not to decide.
- Preserve every holding from the DraftCourtOrder, even if you would personally phrase it differently.

## Operative portion conventions

- Each operative clause is a separate numbered paragraph.
- Use imperative language: "The petition is allowed / dismissed.", "The Respondent is directed to...", "The order dated ___ is quashed and set aside."
- Add costs only if directed in the draft order. Default: "There shall be no order as to costs."
- End the operative portion with "Ordered accordingly." or the equivalent for the jurisdiction.

## Placeholders

When specific information is missing, use tasteful placeholders:
- Judge name: "HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ___"
- Case number: "___ of ____"
- Monetary amounts: "₹___/-"
- Party names or designations: "[Name]"
